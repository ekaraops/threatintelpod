## New Tools, Techniques, and Tactics
*   **OSINT (Open Source Intelligence):** Using public information to gather data on a target before an attack.
*   **Pretexting:** Creating a fake identity or scenario (e.g., posing as a Swedish ambassador) to trick people into granting access.
*   **Physical Penetration Testing:** Testing building security by physically entering premises rather than just hacking networks.
*   **Nmap & Nessus:** Network scanning tools used to identify active devices and known vulnerabilities on a network.
*   **Red Teaming:** A full-scope, multi-layered attack simulation designed to test an organization's detection and response capabilities.
*   **Subsea Data Centers:** A new infrastructure method using watertight, modular containers placed on the ocean floor for cooling and security.

## Detection & Defense Insights
*   **Defense in Depth:** The importance of having multiple layers of security (e.g., fences, guards, badges, and internal network controls).
*   **Physical Security Gaps:** Unlocked windows, unalarmed loading docks, and unsecured perimeter fences can bypass high-tech digital security.
*   **Identity Verification:** The danger of relying on "visual" cues or simple verbal confirmation without strict ID/badge protocols.
*   **Asset Management:** The risk of leaving physical keys (trucks, server racks) in easily accessible places like cup holders or mud flaps.
*   **Critical Infrastructure Risks:** The potential for a single incorrect command in an Industrial Control System (ICS) to disrupt essential services like water.
*   **Insider/Authorized Access Risks:** Even authorized testers can cause accidental outages if they lack strict "rules of engagement."

## Actionable Takeaways
*   **For CISOs:** Ensure your physical security team and IT security team are communicating; a digital firewall cannot stop a person climbing through a window.
*   **For SOC Teams:** Monitor for "out-of-band" signals, such as unexpected physical access or changes in environmental controls.
*   **For Security Leaders:** Implement strict "Rules of Engagement" for all third-party testers to prevent accidental disruption of critical services.
*   **For Physical Security:** Treat physical keys and access badges with the same level of digital rigor as passwords and encryption keys.
*   **For Professionals:** Adopt an "attacker mindset"—don't just ask "is this tool working?" but "how would someone bypass this?"
