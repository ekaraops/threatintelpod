## TL;DR (Too Long, Didn't Read)
*   **Companies are becoming APIs:** In the future, if a company doesn't have an API that an AI can use, that company effectively won't exist for most users.
*   **The Rise of Agentic Infrastructure:** We are moving from humans interacting with software to humans interacting with agents, which then interact with APIs.
*   **Shift from Human to Process:** Companies are evolving from being collections of people to being collections of automated processes and algorithms.
*   **Personal AI Stacks (PI):** Individuals are building "Personal AI Infrastructure" to amplify their own capabilities and manage the overwhelming pace of AI updates.
*   **The "Ideal State" Algorithm:** A new way to use AI is to have it reverse-engineer a vague human request into "discrete, testable criteria" before it ever starts working.
*   **Democratized Quality:** AI agents can now filter massive amounts of information (news, research, social media) to find high-quality content, regardless of the source's fame.

## New Tools, Techniques, and Tactics
*   **Agentic Personal AI (PI) Infrastructure:** A framework for building a unified system of agents to manage a person's digital life and knowledge.
*   **Council Technique:** A workflow where 2–16 custom agents are spun up to debate a topic aggressively to find the best solution.
*   **Iterative Depth:** A research-based technique where an AI is asked the same question from different perspectives to increase its intelligence and thoroughness.
*   **Arbull (Tree):** A method of creating discrete, containerized AI actions (running locally or in the cloud) that can be chained into pipelines or "flows."
*   **Ideal State Criteria:** A tactic where AI defines exactly what a "successful" outcome looks like (testable requirements) before executing a task.
*   **Probability Messing:** A technique for increasing creativity by manually manipulating the output probabilities of an LLM.

## Detection & Defense Insights
*   **Shadow AI/BYOAI:** Enterprises are seeing a "Bring Your Own AI" trend where employees use personal agents at work out of necessity or panic.
*   **Process-Based Security:** Moving toward a model where security is "built-in" via Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) that AI follows, rather than relying on human oversight.
*   **Custom Software Environments:** Security testers may soon encounter completely custom, AI-generated software stacks during assessments, requiring new testing methodologies.
*   **Information Silos/Reality Bubbles:** Defensive teams must be aware that AI agents may filter news and intelligence differently for different people, creating "different realities."

*   **Algorithmic Companies:** Large organizations are shifting from human-led "recommendations" to algorithm-led "rules."
